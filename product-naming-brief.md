# Product Naming Brainstorm — Prompt

> Copy everything below the line into your LLM of choice (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) to brainstorm product names. Edit the bracketed constraints if your thinking evolves.

---

You are a world-class brand strategist and naming expert who has named category-defining B2B SaaS and AI products. I need your help naming a **new, standalone product and brand** from Chargebee.

## Context

Chargebee is a well-known billing and revenue management platform, historically strong in **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** and subscription businesses. We are building something new and separate.

**What we're naming:** A brand-new product with its own brand identity, built for **Sales-Led Growth (SLG)** motions — i.e. complex, negotiated, sales-driven deals rather than self-serve subscriptions.

**Positioning pillars:**
- **AI-native and AI-first.** AI is not a bolt-on feature; it is the core operating model. The product acts as an intelligent collaborator, not just a form.
- **A focused point solution**, not a sprawling suite. It does one hard thing exceptionally well.
- **New brand, new take.** It should feel like a fresh, modern, confident product — endorsed by Chargebee but able to stand on its own (think how "Copilot" stands apart yet belongs to its parent).

**What the product actually does (the core job to be done):**
- A sales rep or deal desk **drops in one or more signed contracts** (messy PDFs from negotiated deals).
- The AI **reads and understands** them, then extracts and structures everything needed to bill: accounts, addresses, contract terms, products & pricing, billing/payment schedules.
- It **matches** the extracted customer to existing records (or creates new ones), flags what needs attention, and **generates billing-ready invoices**.
- Throughout, the AI **comments, suggests, and takes actions** alongside the human in a review "workbench" — a genuine human-AI collaboration.
- Net effect: it collapses the painful gap between **"deal closed"** and **"revenue recognized / customer billed"** — turning sales paperwork into live, accurate billing with minimal manual effort.

**Brand personality (to guide tone of names):**
- Precise, intelligent, fast, trustworthy, and modern.
- Slightly bold and premium — not cutesy, not enterprise-stuffy.
- Visual identity leans on a deep navy, a punchy orange, and an orange→violet "AI gradient" that signals intelligence and momentum.

## Naming Criteria

Score every candidate against these:
1. **Evocative** of speed, intelligence, precision, or the "turn signed deals into revenue" idea — without being a literal, boring description.
2. **Short and brandable** — ideally 1–2 syllables, easy to say, spell, and remember globally.
3. **Ownable** — distinctive, not generic; avoids the tired "AI/Sync/Flow/Hub/Genius" clichés unless used unexpectedly.
4. **Works as a sub-brand** — reads naturally as "[Name] by Chargebee."
5. **Scalable** — won't box the product in if it expands beyond contract ingestion later.
6. **Clean** — no unfortunate meanings in major languages; plausible domain/trademark availability (flag obvious conflicts you're aware of).

## What I want from you

Generate **30 name candidates, organized into 5–6 naming territories/themes** (e.g. "Speed & Momentum," "Intelligence & Insight," "Bridge / Deal-to-Revenue," "Invented/Abstract coined words," "Latin/mythology roots," "Metaphor"). For each territory, give a one-line description of the strategy.

For **each name**, provide:
- **Name**
- **Why it works** (1–2 lines tying it to the product and criteria)
- **Tagline** (a short, punchy one-liner: "[Name] — [promise]")
- **Risk/flag** (any spelling, meaning, or availability concern)

Then finish with:
- **Your top 5 recommendations**, ranked, with a sentence each on why they'd win.
- A few **"wildcard" bold picks** that break convention but could be memorable.

Avoid: overused AI-startup suffixes (-ify, -ly, -.ai gimmicks), names that are hard to pronounce, and anything that just literally says "contract" or "invoice."

Ask me up to 3 clarifying questions first **only if** they would materially change your naming direction; otherwise go straight to the names.
